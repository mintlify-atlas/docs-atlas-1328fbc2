## Understanding Printer Permissions

The app requires several permissions to connect to Bluetooth printers:

- **Bluetooth**: Basic Bluetooth connectivity (Android 11 and below)
- **Bluetooth Scan**: Discover nearby Bluetooth devices (Android 12+)
- **Bluetooth Connect**: Connect to paired devices (Android 12+)
- **Location**: Required by Android for Bluetooth scanning

<Info>
  The app automatically requests these permissions when you first access printer settings.
</Info>

## Step 1: Pair Your Printer with Android

Before the app can connect to your printer, you must pair it through your phone's Bluetooth settings.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Turn on Printer">
    Power on your Bluetooth thermal printer. Most printers indicate readiness with a blinking blue LED.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Open Android Bluetooth Settings">
    From the app's Settings page:
    - Tap on **Print Device** under the Hardware section
    - Tap the **Settings gear icon** in the printer row
    - This opens your phone's Bluetooth settings directly
    
    Alternatively, you can manually go to **Android Settings > Connected devices > Bluetooth**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Find Your Printer">
    In Bluetooth settings, look for your printer in the "Available devices" list.
    
    Common printer names:
    - `BlueTooth Printer`
    - `POS-5805` or `POS-8005`
    - `RPP02N` or `RPP300`
    - `MTP-II` or `MTP-III`
    
    <Tip>
      Check your printer's manual or label for its exact Bluetooth name.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Pair the Device">
    Tap on your printer's name in the available devices list.
    
    - You may be prompted for a PIN code
    - Common default PINs: `0000`, `1234`, or `1111`
    - Check your printer manual if these don't work
    
    Once paired, the printer appears under "Paired devices."
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 2: Connect Printer in the App

After pairing your printer with Android, connect it within the app.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    From the home screen, tap the **Settings** icon in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View Print Device">
    Under the **Hardware** section, you'll see **Print Device**.
    
    The subtitle shows the connection status:
    - `No printer connected` - No active connection
    - `[Printer Name]` - Connected printer name
    - `CONNECTED` badge appears when active
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Tap Refresh">
    Tap the **Refresh icon** (circular arrow) to scan for paired printers.
    
    <Note>
      The refresh button shows a loading spinner while scanning. This typically takes 2-3 seconds.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Automatic Connection">
    The app automatically:
    1. Scans for all Bluetooth-paired devices
    2. Filters for printers (devices with "print" or "POS" in the name)
    3. Attempts to connect to the first compatible printer found
    4. Saves the connection for future use
    
    <Info>
      Once connected, the app remembers your printer and auto-connects on startup and during printing.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Connection Success">
    When successfully connected, you'll see:
    - A green snackbar message: "Connected to printer"
    - The printer name displayed under Print Device
    - A green `CONNECTED` badge next to the name
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How Auto-Connection Works

The app uses intelligent auto-connection to ensure your printer is always ready:

<Steps>
  <Step title="App Startup">
    When you open the app, it checks for a saved printer MAC address.
    
    If found, it attempts to connect automatically in the background.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="During Printing">
    When you tap "Print Receipt" on the checkout page:
    
    1. The app checks if the printer is connected
    2. If not connected, it tries to auto-connect using saved settings
    3. If auto-connect succeeds, printing proceeds
    4. If auto-connect fails, you see an error message
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Connection Persistence">
    The printer's MAC address is stored locally, so you only need to connect once. The app will remember and reconnect automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Receipt Format and ESC/POS Commands

The app uses ESC/POS commands to format receipts on thermal printers.

### Receipt Layout

A printed receipt includes:

1. **Shop Name** - Centered, bold, large font
2. **Address Lines** - Centered, normal font (if configured)
3. **Phone Number** - Centered, normal font
4. **Date/Time** - Format: `DD-MM-YYYY HH:MM AM/PM`
5. **Separator Line** - Dashed line
6. **Column Headers** - "Item", "Price", "Total"
7. **Line Items** - Each product with quantity, price, and total
8. **Total Line** - Right-aligned, bold
9. **Footer Text** - Centered (customizable in Shop Details)
10. **Paper Feed** - Extra lines for easy tearing

### Example Receipt

```
         ELITE GROCERIES
      123 Market Street
       City, State 12345
       Phone: 9876543210
      14-03-2026 02:30 PM
--------------------------------
Item            Price   Total
--------------------------------
2x Basmati Rice 299.00  598.00
1x Cooking Oil  450.00  450.00
--------------------------------
                TOTAL: 1048.00

   Thank you for shopping!
         Visit again!


```

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Printer won't pair in Android settings">
    **Solutions:**
    - Ensure printer is in pairing mode (check manual for button combination)
    - Try restarting both printer and phone
    - Move printer closer to your phone (within 1 meter)
    - Make sure printer isn't already paired with another device
    - Try unpairing and re-pairing if previously connected
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="App can't find printer after pairing">
    **Solutions:**
    - Tap the Refresh button in the app to re-scan
    - Verify the printer is powered on and in range
    - Check that Bluetooth is enabled on your phone
    - Grant all requested permissions (Bluetooth, Location)
    - Try restarting the app
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Connection drops frequently">
    **Solutions:**
    - Keep printer within 5-10 meters of phone
    - Ensure printer has sufficient battery/power
    - Avoid interference from other Bluetooth devices
    - Update your phone's Android version if outdated
    - Try re-pairing the printer
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Print failed error when printing">
    **Possible causes:**
    - Printer is out of paper
    - Printer battery is too low
    - Bluetooth connection was interrupted
    - Printer is offline or turned off
    
    **Solutions:**
    - Check printer paper and reload if empty
    - Charge or plug in printer
    - Tap Refresh in Settings to reconnect
    - Power cycle the printer
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Receipt prints garbled text or symbols">
    **Causes:**
    - Incompatible printer model
    - Incorrect character encoding
    
    **Solutions:**
    - Verify your printer supports ESC/POS commands
    - Test with a different thermal printer if possible
    - Check printer manual for supported command sets
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Receipt prints blank">
    **Common issues:**
    - Thermal paper loaded backwards (thermal side facing wrong way)
    - Expired thermal paper (no longer heat-sensitive)
    - Printer temperature too low
    
    **Solutions:**
    - Flip the paper roll and reload
    - Test with fresh thermal paper
    - Check printer settings/temperature
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
